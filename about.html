About us

We built Code the Future for our own kids first.

A hands-on AI camp and learning platform for kids ages 8–11 in Louisville, Kentucky — so they grow up understanding artificial intelligence instead of being a little afraid of it.

AI is being woven into nearly everything today's kids will grow up to do. They're going to use it either way — the head start is understanding it: how it works, what it's great at, and where it still needs a human.

Our mission

Code the Future teaches AI literacy and modern problem-solving the way the work actually happens now — AI as a reasoning engine inside a real workflow, with humans handling judgment and the exceptions. We don't have kids watch videos about technology. They use real AI: drawing for an AI that actually "sees," training their own mini-models, and building things they're proud of.

The skill that matters in an AI world isn't memorizing answers — it's asking great questions, spotting when an answer is wrong, and knowing when to trust the machine. That's exactly what our game-like missions train.

How it works

  • A weekly 90-minute session at the Louisville public library with a coach who works in the industry.
  • Game-like missions at home on our web platform — 12 missions per module, built around real AI tools.
  • Small founding cohorts so every kid gets seen, with a parent-friendly, ad-free experience.

Built kid-safe, on purpose

Kids join with a cohort code — no email or personal information is required from children. Chat with their cohort passes a profanity filter and is visible to staff, and the whole curriculum was shaped with a child psychologist. You can read the details on our privacy page.
