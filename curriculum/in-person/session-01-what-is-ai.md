# Session 1 — "What even is AI?"  *(companion to Missions 1–4)*

The first Library Session, **90 minutes**. Kids arrive having played Missions 1–4 at home:
what AI is, how brains find patterns, how training works, and the AI-or-Not arcade. Tonight
they live it — review it as a game, build with it, and make something to take home.

**Room setup (10 min before):** two labeled bins up front · AI-or-Not cards hidden around
the room · cracker station covered with a cloth (mystery builds hype) · craft table set for
Build-a-Bot (cardboard, pipe cleaners, googly eyes, tape, markers) · agenda card with the
seven segments visible · badge sticker sheets at the door · a bell or buzzer for the
Showdown.

---

## 0:00–0:08 · Power-Up ⚡
Kids check in, find their name card, and **claim physical badge stickers** for every
mission badge they earned on the platform this week (mentor has the roster from Mission
Control). Stickers go on their name card — it becomes their season badge-board.

Standing circle, fast: *"One thing from the missions that surprised you — fifteen
seconds, go."* (Quiet-entry option: place your sticker, pass with a thumbs-up.)

> Mentor note: this is a brag, not a quiz. There are no wrong answers; "the music
> changes by itself" counts exactly as much as "AI guesses the next word."

## 0:08–0:22 · Mission Recap Showdown 🎮  *(the week's review, as a game show)*
The QA/review — but no one will call it review (activity bank #11). Split into 2–3 teams.
Mentor is the host; teams ring in.

- **Round 1 — Quick Hits (1 pt):** *"AI or just a machine: a toaster."* *"True or false:
  AI learns from examples."* *"Finish the rule: a normal machine follows ___; AI learns
  from ___."* Pull straight from Missions 1–4.
- **Round 2 — Spicy (2 pts):** the tricky ones. *"Your phone camera finding faces — AI or
  machine, and WHY?"* Points only with the *why*.
- **Lightning Round (30 sec):** rapid AI-or-Not, mentor fires items, teams shout, tally.
- **Final Wager:** each team bets points on one big question — *"In your own words, what's
  the ONE difference between AI and a regular machine?"* Best answer takes the pot.

Keep it fast and silly; the scoreboard matters less than every kid hearing the core ideas
five more times. **Land it:** *"You just proved you already get the biggest idea in AI —
machines follow rules, AI learns from examples."*

> Mentor note: seed each team with a strong-and-a-shy kid; the buzzer gives quiet kids a
> no-talking way to compete (ring first, whisper the answer to a teammate who says it).

## 0:22–0:32 · Warm-Up: AI-or-Not Bins 🗑️
Scavenger + sort (activity bank #7). Cards are hidden around the room; kids hunt, then
drop each into **🧠 AI** or **⚙️ JUST A MACHINE**. Quick group review of the spiciest
finds — this both burns arrival energy and keeps the Showdown vocabulary warm.

**The takeaway, said out loud together:** *"If it LEARNS and guesses — it's AI. If it
follows the same fixed steps — it's just a machine."*

## 0:32–1:02 · Main Build: Train the Human Robot 🤖
The headliner (activity bank #1). Reveal the cracker station.

1. Mentor announces: *"I am a robot. I do EXACTLY what your program says. I have no
   common sense. None."*
2. Teams of 3–4 write step-by-step instructions for: **make a peanut-butter cracker**
   (Round 2: **stack the cup pyramid** — there's now time for both).
3. Run each program literally and theatrically. Jar placed ON cracker. Knife used
   handle-first. Chaos. Laughter.
4. Teams **debug** — revise their instructions — and resubmit. Second runs mostly work.
5. Land the lesson while they're still laughing: *"You just did what programmers do all
   day: write, test, fix. And here's the twist — training an AI is showing it examples
   instead of writing every step. You did BOTH this week: you wrote steps for me, and on
   the platform you trained the network by strengthening connections."*

> Allergy note: use a peanut-free spread or switch to the cup pyramid as the primary —
> confirm with parent forms. [KENYA: review food-in-session policy.]

## 1:02–1:20 · Make-It Lab: Build-a-Bot 🛠️
New for the 90-minute hour (activity bank #12). Each kid builds a robot from the craft
table — cardboard body, pipe-cleaner arms, googly eyes, a marker face.

The twist that makes it AI, not just craft: **give your bot exactly ONE job** (write it on
a tag — "sorts my socks," "waters the plant"). Then the room votes on two questions:
*Does it follow fixed rules, or does it LEARN?* and *Could it be unfair if it only saw one
kind of example?* (a soft pre-tease of bias). Bots go home or — folded flat — into the
Time Capsule with a photo.

> This is the segment parents hear about at dinner. Keep supplies cheap and plentiful; the
> learning is in the *one job* tag and the vote, not the glue.

## 1:20–1:28 · Together-Time: Time Capsule, Entry #1 ⏳
Energy down, lights softer. Hand out capsules — *"these get opened in 2031, when you're
in high school."* Tonight's prompt card: **"What is AI, in your own words?"** Write or
draw. No sharing required; capsules are private. Slip the card in (plus the Build-a-Bot
photo or tag).

Then a fast **Teach-It-Back prep**: *"Pick ONE thing from tonight — the robot, the bots,
the Showdown, anything. At pickup, you have to teach it to your grown-up in one minute.
That's your mission."* (Parents consistently report this as the moment they realize what
they paid for.)

## 1:28–1:30 · Mission Briefing 🎬  *(overlaps pickup)*
Movie-trailer voice: *"This week on the platform… your computer learns to SEE. You'll
draw — anything you want — and a real AI will look at your drawing and guess it. Missions
5 through 8 unlock Friday. Also: there's a game where YOU get to be the neural network…
no wait, that's next week, in this room. Forget I said that."*

As kids teach their grown-up their one thing, the mentor floats and says one specific,
positive sentence about each kid to their parent ("Maya's debugging instinct is real — she
found the bug in her team's program twice"). That sentence is the retention engine.

### Materials checklist (Session 1)
- [ ] Badge sticker sheets + name cards
- [ ] Bell/buzzer + small whiteboard for the Showdown scoreboard
- [ ] AI-or-Not card set + 2 bins
- [ ] Crackers, spread (allergy-safe), butter knife, wipes · cups ×15
- [ ] Paper + markers for "programs"
- [ ] Build-a-Bot kit: cardboard, pipe cleaners, googly eyes, tape, markers, job tags
- [ ] Capsules ×(cohort size) + prompt card #1 · phone/printer for bot photos
- [ ] Agenda card · Mission Control roster printout (badge claims)

### Success looks like
Every kid: earned ≥1 physical sticker, scored in the Showdown, touched materials in every
segment, laughed during the Robot, built and named a bot, wrote/drew a capsule entry, and
taught one thing at pickup. Mentor: said one specific sentence to every parent. Zero
minutes of screens.
