# Research — competitive & market landscape

A living record of who else is teaching kids to code and to understand AI — nationally —
across both tangents Jon wants tracked:

- **Online platforms** — kids' coding/CS *and* kids' AI-literacy products (ages ~8–11).
- **In-person** — camps, after-school, franchises, museums, and library programs, and **how
  those trainings are staffed and managed**.
- **The combination + the future of work** — who genuinely fuses *coding + AI* (not a
  bolt-on), and who frames learning around **AI agents** and **how the nature of work is
  changing**. This is the angle Code the Future is betting on.
- **White space + positioning** — where the market is crowded (kids coding) vs. thin (kids
  AI literacy, agent/future-of-work framing, a blended online-platform + local in-person
  community), and how Code the Future differentiates.

## How this is maintained

This branch (`competitive-research`) is the home for market intelligence. Reports are
generated with the deep-research harness (fan-out web search → source fetch → adversarial
verification → cited synthesis) and refreshed as the (fast-moving) kids-AI space changes.
Treat findings as point-in-time and re-verify pricing/feature claims before quoting them
externally.
