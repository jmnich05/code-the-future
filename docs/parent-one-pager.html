Code the Future AI Summer Camp · Louisville · Ages 8–11

Your kid is going to grow up with AI. This summer, they learn to drive it.

Code the Future is a hands-on AI camp where kids don't watch videos about technology — they use real AI: drawing for an AI that actually sees, training mini-models, and building things they're proud of.

A real hour, every week

Small-group, hands-on sessions at the public library in Louisville — mentors in the room, kids building together, out loud.

Designed with a child psychologist

A psychologist who specializes in child development helped build the platform and reviews every module — pacing, confidence, and safety designed for how 8–11 year-olds actually learn.

A platform kids love logging into

Between sessions, campers keep going at home on our own learning platform — paced like a great game, safe by design.

What your child gets on the platform

12 game-like missions — full-screen, one idea at a time, built for busy brains. Sort, train, draw, and play through every concept.

Real AI, really used — kids draw on a canvas and a real AI guesses their picture; the final mission is built with the same AI professionals use.

Badges and character gear — every mission earns custom badge art plus new gear for their animated character: capes, jetpacks, crowns.

Paced like a real camp — missions unlock in waves across the week, so kids can't binge it in a day and always have a reason to come back.

A safe clubhouse — cohort chat behind a two-layer language filter, with staff able to see every message. No email or personal info required from kids.

Built for every learner — one tap and any mission reads itself aloud; focus-friendly study music kids can remix themselves.

Age-appropriate, honest content — real AI history, real concepts, zero hype. Kids finish able to explain AI to the family.

Adults can see progress — staff dashboards track every camper's missions and flag anyone who's stuck, so no kid slips through.

Safety first, by design

Kids join with a cohort code — no email, no personal data. Every chat message passes a profanity filter and is visible to staff.

Small cohorts on purpose

Limited seats per session so every kid gets seen, helped, and celebrated.

What kids learn

What AI really is, how it learns, how it sees and reads, where it's brilliant, where it fails — and why human judgment is their superpower.
